About

So ... the question. What is this all about? Why am I making a website to showcase myself to the online world? That's a very good question. I moved to Los Angeles in November 2021 after completing my M.A. in Philosophy at the American University of Beirut. I started working at an online Jewelry company in Downtown Los Angeles around January of 2022. A few months into my job I realized that I'd be struggling with feeling unhappy and unfulfilled if I didn't work on my career development.

As a shy and deeply introspective person, I spent most of my free time in college reading, exploring various interests and engaging with other students with life's big questions. I struggled a lot with my mental health in my time in college and found solace in reading novels, watching movies and listening to music. I often turned to art, therapy and spending time with friends as a means to deal with my personal struggles. One day I took a course on Ethics in my sophomore year as a political studies major and I felt like a whole world opened up to me. I felt as though I had found what it is I wanted to do with the rest of my life.

I didn't know that there was a discipline that could combine my desire for intellectual rigor and creative expression. I was an avid reader of fiction, yet I hesitated to study English Literature because I didn't want to turn my passion for reading into an object of study and I didn't see myself as someone pursuing a career as an English professor or teacher.

I also knew that I was good at problem solving. I excelled at math in highschool and freshman year of college. Although no one forced me into it, I felt pressured to pursue Mechanical Engineering in college because of how good I was in math. A lot of the other smart students in my school would major either in a subfield of engineering or pre-med. I also sensed that there was a kind of status symbol in getting accepted in a good engineering program. So once I got accepted into the Engineering program after my sophomore year, I felt like I had 'made it'.

Unfortunately, this is where I started experiencing my first severe symptoms of depression. While I had some feelings of being bummed out and isolated in my freshman year, it wasn't until my sophomore year studying Mechanical Engineering, that I started to feel really depressed. I remember something about 17.5 credits, something to the equivalent of 6 courses + a lab in my first semester. It was a lot to take in and I felt as though my freshman program did not prepare me for the Mechanical Engineering courses. I also felt very isolated. I put a lot of pressure on myself to study for my courses that I ended up isolating myself. It wasn't all that bad from the beginning, but I remember feeling really bad about myself when I showed up to a calculus midterm and found myself unable to solve most of the problems. I ended up leaving the test early after only solving a few problems.

Needless to say, I wasn't the only student who was strugling with their courses. Nor the only freshman struggling to adapt to the rigours of sophomore engineering courses. I just felt like I wasn't good enough and the stress, loneliness and sadness made it impossible for me to study. I ended up withdrawing from the semester. I re-enrolled in the Spring semester, but I ended up dropping the engineering courses then too. I kept the two English Literature courses I took and kept attending them right up until the end. While I did struggle to concentrate, I was able to engage in class and the material we were reading. Taking those two English courses also helped me interact with other students and made me feel like I still was a real student.

Yet again, I unfortunately was not able to finish the final papers for either course. I withdrew from one English course and got a failing grade on the other. It was clear that something was wrong. As that semester came to a close, I felt so bad about withdrawing from my engineering courses that semester and not being able to pass my two English courses that I tried to jump from the fourth floor of my dorm's balcony. It wasn't the first time I've had suicidal thoughts, but it was the first time I've felt so bad that I physically wanted to end my life. I remember what happened when I approached the balcony and tried to lean against it. Images of my mom and and sister started appearing before my mind's eye. I couldn't do it. I went back to my room and cried in my bed. I checked myself into a psych ward the next morning.
