Los Angeles is a Whore (Part 2)

“What are you looking for?” 

Los Angeles, I can hear Varant’s voice through the laptop speakers. “What are you looking for?”. This was weeks after I asked her out and started sitting in the Zendo to keep myself busy and preoccupied with the practice. You see Los Angeles, I couldn’t do anything but think of her and the loneliness and longing were so strong that the car rides home were painful and spending any time alone in a room was an opportunity to think about her and her boyfriend fucking. So I started sitting in the Zendo. It was around the same time the first Ango started and I sat. I sat for two hours after work from 7pm to 9pm. I remember the first time I sat next to Sylvain on the first day of my Ango and almost immediately I started breathing fast and I could feel myself trying to suppress what felt like heavy sudden breathing. I got up from my cushion and bowed to the Shoji and stepped out onto the Zendo porch trying to catch my breath. I thought to myself “I must really like this girl”. I went back in and I bowed to the Shoji and sat back on my cushion next to Sylvain. 

This was the beginning of my most consistent and intense Zen practice. I cried a lot. I couldn’t stop seeing images of her in my mind’s eye even when I kept my eyes open. I kept seeing her face, the smile when she approached me in the rave she invited me to. The dress she wore, her camera. I cried so much.

I feel embarrassed writing about this now, Los Angeles. Doesn’t  writing about this make it harder for me to move on? Am I not lingering and obsessing a little too much over this? Or will writing about it and thinking about it help me process it more so that I can fully understand myself and why I keep finding myself in these situations with girls. How much introspection is healthy and how much is pathological? I don’t know. But you saw me in those days coming home from work. Making my dinner, taking my shower and putting on my robes to head to the Zendo to sit. I started sitting on Saturday mornings. It was hard to get used to the different rituals and the order of bows at first. I didn’t even chant some of the chants they would chant in the morning so I had to get used to the rhythm and pace of those chants. 

I started talking to my new roommate Sylvain who moved in shortly before Ango. I asked him if Zen practice helps solve your problems. He asked me to be more concrete and I told him about my unrequited feelings for my coworker. We talked and talked. We talked even when we went on tangents and didn’t make sense. I don’t think we were supposed to be talking during Ango. But we talked and even he tried to restrain himself and told me that he was like a drug addict when it came to words. But we talked. That was the beginning of my friendship with Sylvain.

I did not think I had it in me to hold down a job I didn't like for this long. I did not think I had it in me to ask out a girl that I liked that I work with. I did not think I had it in me to move to Los Angeles without a plan, without a job or an apartment. I honestly can't remember how I felt before I left. I must have not really felt or anticipated what was in store for me because of the craziness of the past few years before I moved. The truth is that I was unhappy long before I left Lebanon. I felt a great deep sadness and emptiness that I couldn't let go of and I knew it had something to do with my tendency to isolate myself. My grandma told me that other day that I used to smile and laugh all the time when I was a baby. Now that I'm an adult I look sad and worried because I'm starting to see how hard life really is. But she didn't know how sad I was before I moved Los Angeles. She didn't know that I tried to jump from the fourth floor of my dorm building when I was a sophomore in college. She didn't know that I met a guy in college the night before he died by suicide. She didn't know that I told him when we were in his dorm room that I tried to jump from the fourth floor of my dorm building. She didn't know that when I found out that he 'slipped' from the 4th floor of his dorm building I felt bad because I thought I had given him the idea to kill himself that way. 

I really thought I was weak and useless all those years in college. I knew I was smart and that I could write well because I spent so much time reading books and writing down my thoughts, but I didn't think I could follow through with a long-term goal because deep down I really didn't know what I wanted to do. All I knew was that I wanted to earn a college degree just so that I can tell myself I am capable of earning a college degree and to make myself just a little bit more 'relevant’ or ‘competitive’ in the job market. God forbid you enter the job market without a college degree. You'll be exploited with low wages for the rest of your life.

I read a great deal Los Angeles. I read a lot. You don’t understand how much I've read. I read books like there was tomorrow. Like I didn't have any goals or desire to form meaningful relationships with people or a career. I lived my life as if I were already planning on being dead by the time I would be 30. I must have been really insecure about not having any career goals or plans outside of college. I remember struggling a lot with anxiety, sadness and anger, this general feeling that my life wasn't really going anywhere and I didn't want it to go anywhere.
